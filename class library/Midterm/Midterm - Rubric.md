# MIDTERM - Rubric

**Total: 100 points**

This rubric is designed to reward:
- clarity
- real progress
- believable execution
- visible proof
- professional team participation

It does **not** reward hype, fluff, or vague promises.

---

## 1. Problem, Users, and Value Proposition — 20 points
How clearly does the team explain:
- the problem
- the target users
- why the problem matters
- why this solution is worth attention

### 17–20
- problem is specific and easy to understand
- users are clearly defined
- value proposition is believable and focused
- audience can quickly understand why the project matters

### 13–16
- problem and users are mostly clear
- value proposition is understandable but somewhat broad or generic

### 9–12
- problem or users are vague
- explanation feels incomplete or generic
- audience has to guess why the project matters

### 0–8
- problem is unclear
- users are unclear
- project purpose is hard to understand

---

## 2. Product Clarity and Demo Credibility — 25 points
How well does the team show:
- what the product does
- the most important feature or core flow
- a working demo or strong backup demo
- a believable current product state

### 22–25
- product is explained clearly
- demo is focused and understandable
- main flow works or backup is strong and smooth
- demo clearly supports the pitch

### 17–21
- product is understandable
- demo mostly works or backup is usable
- some parts are unclear, rough, or weakly explained

### 11–16
- demo is confusing, too broad, or weakly connected to the pitch
- team tries to show too much or too little
- current product state is hard to judge

### 0–10
- no clear demo
- broken or missing demo with weak backup
- audience cannot tell what works

---

## 3. Evidence of Real Progress and Technical Readiness — 20 points
How well does the team prove:
- real progress
- repo-backed work
- Sprint 2 alignment
- testing / checking effort
- Definition of Done thinking

### 17–20
- claims are supported by clear evidence
- repo progress is visible and believable
- testing / checking is explained clearly
- team shows real readiness, not just plans

### 13–16
- evidence exists but is uneven or incomplete in places
- some claims are supported clearly, others less so

### 9–12
- evidence is weak, hard to verify, or too general
- team seems to have progress, but proof is thin

### 0–8
- very little proof
- audience must guess what is real
- progress is mostly described, not shown

---

## 4. Roadmap and Feasibility — 15 points
How realistic is the team’s roadmap from now to the Final and beyond?

### 13–15
- roadmap is realistic and prioritized
- Final target is believable
- post-Final ideas are sensible and clearly separated from near-term work

### 10–12
- roadmap mostly makes sense
- some priorities or dates are still fuzzy

### 7–9
- roadmap exists but feels vague, too large, or weakly connected to current progress

### 0–6
- roadmap is unrealistic, unclear, or missing
- team cannot explain how they will get from now to the Final

---

## 5. Presentation Quality and Team Participation — 20 points
How professionally does the team present?

This includes:
- structure
- clarity
- timing
- visual coherence
- transitions
- visible participation from all members
- ability to handle Q&A

### 17–20
- presentation is organized and on time
- slides and brochure support the message well
- all team members participate visibly
- Q&A responses are clear and thoughtful

### 13–16
- presentation is mostly clear and professional
- team participation is present but somewhat uneven
- timing or transitions may be a little rough

### 9–12
- presentation is disorganized, rushed, or hard to follow in places
- team participation is weak or uneven
- Q&A responses are weak or overly vague

### 0–8
- presentation is hard to follow
- one person carries nearly everything
- multiple team members have no visible role
- timing is poor or Q&A readiness is very weak

---

# Point Summary
- **Problem, Users, and Value Proposition** — 20
- **Product Clarity and Demo Credibility** — 25
- **Evidence of Real Progress and Technical Readiness** — 20
- **Roadmap and Feasibility** — 15
- **Presentation Quality and Team Participation** — 20

**Total = 100 points**

---

# Score Interpretation

## 90–100
Strong Midterm. The project feels real, credible, and well supported.

## 80–89
Good Midterm. Clear progress, but some areas need tightening.

## 70–79
Mixed Midterm. Important pieces are present, but clarity, proof, or execution are weak.

## 60–69
Weak Midterm. Team has some progress, but too much is unclear, unsupported, or poorly presented.

## Below 60
Not yet at Midterm standard. The team did not show enough clarity, proof, or readiness.

---

# Notes for Students
A high score does **not** require a finished product.

A high score requires:
- a clear story
- real evidence
- one believable demo
- a realistic roadmap
- visible team participation

The Midterm should feel like a team presenting a real project that is moving forward, not a team describing a wish list.
