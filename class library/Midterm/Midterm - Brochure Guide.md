# MIDTERM - Brochure Guide

## Purpose
The brochure is a **project handout**, not a printed copy of your slide deck.

Its job is to help the audience quickly understand:
- what your project is
- who it is for
- what it does
- what is already real
- where the project is going next

A good brochure should be easy to scan, visually clean, and useful even if someone looks at it after your presentation.

---

## Format
Your brochure may be:
- printed,
- shared digitally through a QR code,
- or both.

### Length
Recommended length: **4 to 6 pages**

That is enough space to communicate clearly without turning the brochure into a full report.

---

## Required Content
Your brochure must include all of the following:

### 1. Project Name and Tagline
A clear name and a short line that explains the project.

### 2. Problem
What problem are you solving?

### 3. Target Users
Who is this for?

### 4. Solution / Product Overview
What does the product do?

### 5. Core Features
What are the most important features?

### 6. Current Progress
What is already built or working now?

### 7. Timeline / Roadmap
Show:
- what will be completed by the Final
- what comes after the Final

### 8. Team Roles
Show the team members and their roles.

### 9. Ask
Include a short, believable ask.

Examples:
- pilot users
- mentorship
- technical support
- funding for next-stage development
- partnership opportunities

### 10. QR Code / Key Links
Include a QR code or key links to:
- the project
- the demo
- the repo
- the brochure itself if digital

---

## Recommended Page Structure

### Page 1 — Cover
Include:
- project name
- tagline
- one short summary paragraph
- visual or mockup if useful
- QR code

### Page 2 — Problem and Users
Include:
- the problem
- who experiences it
- why it matters

### Page 3 — Solution and Core Features
Include:
- what your product does
- key features
- what makes it useful

### Page 4 — Current Progress
Include:
- what is working now
- what the current demo shows
- what has been completed so far

### Page 5 — Timeline / Roadmap
Include:
- now to Midterm
- Midterm to Final
- after Final

### Page 6 — Team, Ask, and Links
Include:
- team members and roles
- your ask
- repo/demo/site links
- QR code if not already included clearly

---

## Design Rules
Your brochure should feel like a brochure, not a report.

### Good brochure characteristics
- short sections
- clear headings
- easy scanning
- useful visuals
- balanced whitespace
- readable text
- simple, professional layout

### Avoid
- giant paragraphs
- tiny text
- walls of bullet points
- copying slide text directly
- long technical explanations
- too many colors or visual styles
- vague marketing language with no substance

---

## Writing Rules
Keep the writing:
- clear
- short
- specific
- believable

Use plain language.

Write like you are introducing a real project to someone who has never seen it before.

---

## Common Mistakes
Avoid these:
- making the brochure just a mini version of the slide deck
- describing future plans without showing current progress
- forgetting the roadmap
- forgetting the ask
- forgetting QR / useful links
- writing too much
- making the design crowded or hard to read

---

## What Good Looks Like
A strong brochure:
- explains the project quickly
- supports the pitch without repeating it word-for-word
- shows that the team understands its users, progress, and next steps
- feels polished enough to hand to a real audience

---

## Submission
Teams must submit the brochure as part of the Midterm package.

Accepted forms:
- PDF
- brochure webpage with QR code
- printable digital layout

If digital, make sure the QR code or link works before the presentation.
