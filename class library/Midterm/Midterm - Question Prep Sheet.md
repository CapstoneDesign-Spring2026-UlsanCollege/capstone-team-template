# MIDTERM - Question Prep Sheet

## Purpose
The Midterm includes **8 minutes of pitch** and **2 minutes of Q&A**.

During Q&A, the instructor may continue asking questions until time is up.

Your team should be ready to answer clearly, briefly, and honestly.

Do not memorize long speeches.
Prepare short, believable answers.

---

## What Good Answers Sound Like
Good answers are:
- clear
- specific
- honest
- connected to what is already real

Avoid answers that are:
- vague
- too long
- full of hype
- disconnected from the current state of the project

---

## 20 Questions You Should Be Ready to Answer

## Problem / Users
1. What exact problem are you solving?
2. Who has this problem most strongly?
3. Why does this problem matter enough to solve?
4. What are people doing now instead of using your product?
5. Why is your solution better than the current alternative?

## Product / Solution
6. What does your product do in one sentence?
7. What is the single most important feature right now?
8. What part of the product is already working today?
9. What part is still weak or unfinished?
10. If you had to cut one feature, what would you cut first?

## Demo / Build Reality
11. What exactly did your team build so far?
12. What evidence shows this is real progress, not just planning?
13. What breaks most easily right now?
14. How have you tested the feature you showed?
15. If the live demo fails, what is your backup?

## Feasibility / Execution
16. Can this realistically be finished to a solid Final level?
17. What is the biggest technical risk right now?
18. What is the biggest team or process risk right now?
19. Why are you confident your roadmap is realistic?
20. What would you do next if this project continued after the course?

---

## How to Prepare
For each question, your team should decide:
- who answers first
- what the short answer is
- what proof or example supports the answer

Try to prepare answers that are about:
- 1 to 3 sentences long
- easy to understand
- connected to your real current project

---

## Weak vs Strong Answers

### Weak
“We are still working on a lot of things, but we plan to improve everything later.”

### Stronger
“Our biggest weak point right now is account recovery. The main user flow works, but that part is not stable yet, so it is not in the Midterm demo.”

### Weak
“We tested it locally and it worked.”

### Stronger
“We tested the signup flow with one valid case and two invalid cases, then confirmed the user reached the dashboard only when the input was valid.”

### Weak
“We think we can finish by the Final.”

### Stronger
“We cut two lower-priority features and focused on the booking flow. Based on our current progress, we think that is realistic by the Final.”

---

## Rules for Good Q&A
- answer the question directly
- keep answers short
- do not panic if your project is still rough
- be honest about weak points
- do not pretend unfinished work is complete
- connect answers to proof when possible

---

## Team Prep Checklist
- [ ] all team members reviewed the 20 questions
- [ ] each question has a likely first responder
- [ ] weak areas have honest answers prepared
- [ ] testing/checking answers are ready
- [ ] roadmap answers are realistic
- [ ] backup demo answer is ready
- [ ] ask / next-step answer is ready

---

## Final Reminder
A good Q&A does not require a perfect project.

It requires a team that understands:
- the problem
- the product
- the progress
- the risks
- the next steps

If your team can answer clearly and honestly, your project will feel much more real.
