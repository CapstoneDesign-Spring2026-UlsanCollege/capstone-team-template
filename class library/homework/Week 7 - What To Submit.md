# WEEK 7 - What to Submit

## Purpose
Week 7 is about turning your Sprint 2 plan into a **clearer, more believable Midterm pitch**.

This week is not about adding random new features.
This week is about making your project easier to:
- explain
- demo
- trust
- rehearse

Your goal is to prepare a **draft Midterm deck** and strengthen the **story, demo, and evidence** behind it.

---

## Important Naming Rule
Use these names correctly:

- **Sprint 2** = the multi-week sprint leading to the Midterm
- **Week 7 Packet** = this week’s weekly submission

Do **not** call the weekly packet a sprint.

---

## What to Submit for the Week 7 Packet
Your team submits **one GitHub Issue** titled:

**Weekly Sprint Packet — Week 7**

Your packet must include all of the following.

---

## 1. Demo Link + Updated Script + Backup Plan
Include:
- a live demo link, video link, or current working proof
- an updated **3-bullet demo script**
- a backup plan if the main demo fails

### Example
- User opens the app and signs in
- User completes the main task
- User sees the expected result

Backup plan:
- recorded walkthrough video
- narrated screenshots
- backup hosted version

---

## 2. Midterm Deck Draft Link
Include a link to your **draft Midterm presentation**.

Your draft does **not** need to be visually perfect yet.
It does need to be clear enough that someone can understand:
- the problem
- the user
- the solution
- the main demo flow
- what works now
- what comes next

---

## 3. Midterm Story Spine
Write short answers for these:
- **Problem** — What problem are you solving?
- **User** — Who has this problem?
- **Solution** — What does your project do?
- **Main demo flow** — What is the one main thing you will show?
- **Proof** — What can you show that proves the project is real?
- **Roadmap** — What is the next realistic step before Final?
- **Ask** — What feedback, support, or approval are you asking for?

Keep this short and direct.

---

## 4. Speaker Roles + Handoff Plan
Your packet must say:
- who opens
- who explains the problem/user
- who sets up or runs the demo
- who explains the roadmap
- who closes with the ask

Also include **one short handoff line** between speakers.

### Example
- Minji explains the problem and target user
- Joon explains the solution and demo setup
- Haneul runs the demo
- Ara explains the roadmap and ask

Handoff line:
> Now that you understand the problem, Joon will show how our app solves it.

---

## 5. What Works Now / Proof
Show real proof of progress.

Include links to things like:
- Issues
- PRs
- commits
- screenshots
- CI runs
- updated docs
- board snapshot
- demo video
- deployed version

Rule:
**If it isn’t linked, it didn’t happen.**

---

## 6. Docs Update
Link the project docs you updated this week.

At minimum, this should include:
- `SPRINT_2.md`
- any updated planning, testing, design, or architecture docs
- any other repo pages that now better support your Midterm story

Your docs should match what you plan to say in the Midterm.

---

## 7. Board Snapshot
Show your board with:
- **To Do / Doing / Done**
- owners visible
- Midterm-related work clearly visible

Your board should make it easy to see:
- what is finished
- what is being prepared for the Midterm
- what is still risky

---

## 8. Current Risks / Weak Points
Include short, honest notes on:
- what still feels weak
- what could fail during the demo
- what is still unclear in the deck
- what your team must fix before the Midterm

Do not pretend everything is fine if it is not.
A small honest risk list is much better than fake confidence.

---

## 9. Individual Contribution Receipts
Each student must add a comment with **2–3 contribution receipts**.

Good receipts include:
- PR links
- Issue links
- commits
- review comments
- screenshots with context
- CI runs
- doc update links

Do not just write “I helped.”
Show proof.

---

## 10. This Week’s Engineering Practice Spine
This week’s engineering practice is:

**Demo reliability + evidence clarity**

Your Week 7 Packet should show evidence that your team improved:
- demo stability
- backup readiness
- speaker clarity
- repo proof

---

## Common Mistakes
Avoid these:
- too many slides
- too many features in the story
- weak or missing backup plan
- no clear speaker order
- no proof slide or evidence links
- confusing roadmap
- ending without an ask
- packet claims that are not linked
