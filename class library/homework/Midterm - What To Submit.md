# MIDTERM - What to Submit

## Purpose
The Midterm is your chance to show that your project is **real, focused, and worth continuing**.

You do **not** need a finished product.
But you do need a project that feels believable.

That means your Midterm should show:
- a clear problem
- a clear user
- a clear solution
- a believable main demo flow
- real proof of progress
- a realistic roadmap
- a clear ask

---

## What the Midterm Is
The Midterm is an **investor-style project pitch** supported by:
- a presentation deck
- a working demo or believable backup demo
- a brochure
- repo evidence

Your audience should leave thinking:
- this team understands the problem
- this team has built something real
- this team knows what comes next

---

## What to Submit
Your team must submit all of the following.

---

## 1. Midterm Presentation Deck
Submit a link to your **Midterm deck**.

Your deck should clearly cover:
1. project title / team
2. problem
3. target user
4. solution overview
5. main demo flow
6. what works now / proof
7. roadmap to Final
8. team roles
9. ask

Keep it focused.
Do not overload your deck with extra features or too much text.

---

## 2. Demo Link + Demo Script + Backup Plan
Submit:
- a live demo link, video link, or working proof
- a short **3-bullet demo script**
- a backup plan if the live demo fails

### Example demo script
- User opens the app and signs in
- User completes the main task
- User sees the expected result

### Backup plan examples
- recorded walkthrough video
- narrated screenshots
- backup hosted version

A small stable demo is better than a large broken one.

---

## 3. Midterm Brochure
Submit a link to your **Midterm brochure**.

Your brochure should help a reader quickly understand:
- what the project is
- who it is for
- why it matters
- what works now
- what comes next
- how to access more information

Your brochure should match your deck story.
Do not tell one story in the brochure and a different story in the presentation.

---

## 4. Sprint 2 Document
Submit a link to:
- `SPRINT_2.md`

This document should reflect your current project direction.
It should clearly match what you present in the Midterm.

---

## 5. What Works Now / Proof
Show real proof of progress.

Include links to things like:
- Issues
- PRs
- commits
- screenshots
- CI runs
- deploy link
- board snapshot
- updated docs
- demo video

Rule:
**If it isn’t linked, it didn’t happen.**

---

## 6. Realistic Roadmap
Include a short roadmap that explains:
- what is working now
- what will be improved next
- what you expect to finish by Final
- what you are intentionally not promising yet

Be realistic.
A believable small roadmap is much stronger than a fantasy roadmap.

---

## 7. Speaker Roles
Your Midterm submission must show:
- who opens
- who explains the problem/user
- who explains the solution
- who runs the demo
- who explains the roadmap
- who closes with the ask

Each team member should have a clear role.

---

## 8. Ask
Your team must end with a clear **ask**.

Good asks include:
- feedback on feature priority
- advice on technical direction
- feedback on user experience
- approval to continue with the current scope
- help identifying the next most important improvement

Do not end with a vague thank-you and nothing else.

---

## 9. Team Evidence and Participation
Your repo should make team participation visible.

That means the Midterm should be supported by:
- issues with owners
- PRs showing real work
- docs that match the project direction
- visible board activity
- proof that multiple team members contributed

---

## Common Mistakes
Avoid these:
- trying to show too many features
- talking more about future plans than current progress
- weak or missing backup demo plan
- no proof slide or evidence links
- brochure and deck not matching
- unclear speaking order
- unrealistic roadmap
- ending without a clear ask

---

## What good looks like
A strong Midterm feels like this:
- the problem is easy to understand
- the target user is clear
- the main demo flow is focused
- the project has visible proof behind it
- the roadmap feels realistic
- the team looks prepared and organized

The goal is not to look finished.
The goal is to look **real, focused, and worth supporting**.

---

## Final Reminder
For the Midterm, your team does **not** need to prove that everything is done.

You do need to prove that:
- you chose the right priorities
- you built something believable
- you can explain it clearly
- you have evidence to support your claims
- you know what comes next
