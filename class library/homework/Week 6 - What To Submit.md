# WEEK 6 - What to Submit

## Purpose
Week 6 starts **Sprint 2**, which ends with the **Midterm Pitch**.

This week is about making **one midterm-critical feature** clearly defined, tested, and easy to verify.

Your goal is **not** to build everything.
Your goal is to make one important part of your project feel **real, stable, and believable**.

---

## Important Naming Rule
Starting now, use these names:

- **Sprint 2** = the multi-week sprint leading to the Midterm
- **Week 6 Packet** = this week’s weekly submission

Do **not** call the weekly packet a sprint.

---

## What to Submit for the Week 6 Packet
Your team submits **one GitHub Issue** titled:

**Weekly Sprint Packet — Week 6**

Your packet must include all of the following.

---

## 1. Demo Link + Script + Backup Plan
Include:
- a live demo link, video link, or current working proof
- a short **3-bullet demo script**
- a backup plan if the main demo fails

### Example
- User opens the app and logs in
- User completes the main task
- User sees the expected result on screen

Backup plan:
- recorded walkthrough video
- narrated screenshots

---

## 2. Board Snapshot
Show your board with:
- **To Do / Doing / Done**
- owners visible
- the most important Issue for this week visible

Your board should make it easy to understand what is actually happening.

---

## 3. Sprint Notes
Include short notes on:
- what shipped
- what broke
- what you will do next
- current risks or blockers

Keep this short and honest.

---

## 4. Midterm-Critical Feature
Choose **one** feature or user flow that matters most for the Midterm.

Your packet must say:
- what the feature is
- why it matters
- who owns it first
- why it is realistic for the Midterm

---

## 5. Definition of Done
Write a short, real **Definition of Done** for the midterm-critical feature.

Your DoD should explain:
- what must work
- what result the user should see
- how the team will know it is done
- what proof will be linked

---

## 6. Testing / Checking Plan
Explain **how this feature will be tested or checked this week**.

This can include:
- manual test steps
- basic automated tests
- API checks
- form/input checks
- expected output checks

Be specific.

Bad example:
- tested it locally

Better example:
- created an account, logged in, submitted a post, refreshed the page, and confirmed the post still appeared

---

## 7. Evidence Links
Your packet must link to real proof.

Include links to things like:
- Issues
- PRs
- commits
- screenshots
- CI runs
- demo video
- deployed version

Rule:
**If it isn’t linked, it didn’t happen.**

---

## 8. Student Contribution Receipts
Each student must add a comment with **2–3 contribution receipts**.

Good receipts include:
- PR links
- Issue links
- review comments
- commits
- screenshots with context
- CI runs

Do not just write “I helped.”
Show the proof.

---

## 9. Naming Cleanup Confirmation
Confirm that your team fixed the naming system:
- `SPRINT_2.md` exists
- this week’s issue is titled correctly
- confusing “weekly sprint” wording has been removed where needed

---

## 10. Engineering Practice Spine
This week’s engineering practice is:

**Testing basics + Definition of Done**

Your Week 6 Packet should show evidence that your team worked on this.

---

## Common Mistakes
Avoid these:
- vague demo links
- missing backup plan
- no owner for the main feature
- weak or fake Definition of Done
- “How tested” left blank
- packet claims with no links
- confusing Week 6 and Sprint 2

This week is about making **one important feature**:
- clear

That is what moves your team toward a strong Midterm.
