# SPRINT 2 - Guide to Midterm

## Purpose
**Sprint 2** is the multi-week sprint that leads to your **Midterm Pitch**.

This sprint is not about adding random features.
It is about taking your project from “we have ideas and pieces” to “we can clearly explain it, show it, and prove real progress.”

By the end of Sprint 2, your team should be ready to give a believable investor-style presentation supported by a demo, a brochure, and repo evidence.

For full Midterm requirements, read:
- `MIDTERM - Working Demo Pitch Spec`
- `MIDTERM - Rubric`
- `MIDTERM - Brochure Guide`

---

## Important Naming Rule
Use these terms correctly:

- **Sprint 2** = the multi-week sprint leading to the Midterm
- **Week 6 Packet / Week 7 Packet / Week 8 Packet** = the weekly submission issues

Do **not** call the weekly packet a sprint.

### Required names
- `SPRINT_2.md`
- `Weekly Sprint Packet — Week 6`
- `Weekly Sprint Packet — Week 7`
- `Weekly Sprint Packet — Week 8`

---

## What Sprint 2 Is Trying to Achieve
Sprint 2 should result in a project that is:
- easier to explain
- easier to demo
- more reliable
- better tested or checked
- supported by clear repo evidence
- realistic about what will be finished by the Final

This sprint should move your team from **rough progress** to **credible progress**.

---

## How Sprint 2 Is Different from the Weekly Packet
### Sprint 2
Sprint 2 is the larger build phase.
It is the big picture.
It ends at the Midterm.

### Weekly Packet
The Week Packet is the weekly report and evidence trail.
It shows what happened this week.

### Simple rule
- Sprint 2 = the larger milestone
- Week Packet = the weekly proof

## Weekly Focus

## Week 6 — Define, Clean Up, Harden
This week is about focus and correction.

Your team should:
- fix naming confusion
- identify one midterm-critical feature or flow
- write a real Definition of Done
- create a real testing/checking plan
- clean up board ownership and issue clarity
- start hardening the most important feature

### End of Week 6 target
By the end of Week 6, your team should be able to say:
- this is our most important Midterm feature
- this is what done means
- this is how we tested or checked it
- this is where the proof is linked

---

## Week 7 — Build, Test, Rehearse
This week is about turning the plan into something stable enough to present.

Your team should:
- keep building the midterm-critical flow
- fix obvious weak points
- improve testing/checking
- prepare the deck
- prepare the brochure
- prepare the backup demo
- rehearse the presentation and role handoffs

### End of Week 7 target
By the end of Week 7, your team should have:
- a clearer demo
- a draft presentation
- a draft brochure
- a realistic roadmap
- clearer speaking and presentation roles

---

## Week 8 — Deliver the Pitch
This week is about showing that the project is real, progressing, and worth supporting.

Your team should be ready to present:
- the problem
- the users
- the product
- what works now
- the demo
- the roadmap
- the team roles
- the ask

### End of Week 8 target
Deliver a Midterm Pitch that is:
- clear
- believable
- focused
- supported by evidence

---

## What Must Be True by the Midterm
By the Midterm, your team must have:
- a clear project story
- a believable main demo flow
- a backup demo plan
- a brochure
- a realistic roadmap to the Final and beyond
- visible team participation
- repo evidence supporting your claims

You do **not** need a finished product.
But you do need a project that feels real.

---

## What to Focus On During Sprint 2
Prioritize these:

### 1. One clear main flow
Choose the most important user flow and make it more stable.

### 2. Clear issues and ownership
Your board should show who is doing what.

### 3. Definition of Done
Every important issue should have a real finish line.

### 4. Testing / checking
You need some way to prove that the important feature works.

### 5. Evidence
Your repo, Issues, PRs, board, and packets should make your progress easy to verify.

### 6. Midterm communication
Your team must be able to explain the project clearly, not just build pieces of it.

---

## What to Avoid During Sprint 2
Avoid these common mistakes:
- adding too many new features
- working on low-priority polish instead of the core flow
- weak issue titles and vague tasks
- fake or empty “How tested” notes
- unclear ownership
- confusing weekly work with sprint work
- making a huge roadmap that does not match reality
- waiting too long to prepare the presentation or brochure

---

## If Your Team Is Behind
If your team is behind, do not panic and do not pretend.

Do this instead:

### 1. Cut scope immediately
Identify the smallest believable core flow for the Midterm.

### 2. Stop adding extras
Ignore “nice to have” features for now.

### 3. Make one thing work better
A small working demo beats a large broken one.

### 4. Make the board honest
Do not keep fake “Doing” tasks alive forever.

### 5. Strengthen the evidence trail
If your team has done work, make it easier to verify.

### 6. Prepare the backup early
If the product is fragile, make a strong backup demo now.

### 7. Be realistic in the roadmap
Do not promise a fantasy Final.
Show a believable plan.

Being behind is a problem.
Pretending you are not behind is a much bigger problem.
